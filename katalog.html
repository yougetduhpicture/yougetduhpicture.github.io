KataLog

  • Time: Spring 2021
  • Team: 4 people
  • My role: Benchmarking, Service design, Making the end presentation video (TikTok)
  • Tools: Miro, Microsoft Word, Adobe InDesign, TikTok

Assignment Description

We had free hands to develop a concept for a digital service of our liking. The end result had to be a free form advertisement (not written form), a report describing the product and the process, and a product card summarising it.

The Planning process:

One of the team members had an idea for a digital service, that lets it’s users to list their property. The idea was also that people could lend their property through the service. The whole planning was done on a Miro-board.


We developed the idea together as a team. We had sessions in which we came together and worked on the ideas. We also made a word document in which we had all the info in more detail. It also had more in depth analysis on subjects needed when developing a digital service. The subjects were: Service Product, Target Groups, The Special Features of a Service, Competition and Protection, Lifecycle, Price, Extra- and supporting services, Internal Productisation, Product Card, and Branding.


In the end the service included also a feature for renting the property. We also planned that the platform could collaborate with different services such as item delivery services, insurance companies, and storage services.


The main source of income we planned for KataLog was advertising.

The Digital Service

In the heart of our service was circular economy. The idea of being able to lend someone’s items for a deposit was the main idea. We wanted the service to be as benefitial to users as possible. So that people could do more with less.

Making the video:

The idea was that KataLog would use influencer marketing as a way to reach the audience during the covid pandemic. So we decided that we would make TikTok videos advertising the service as fictional influencers. I made a TikTok-video as RAZ a fictional TikTok musician. I showed off my collection of instruments. I filmed myself with my phone. I filmed a take of myself speaking through a script I made. It took a lot of takes to get it right. Later I realised I could have recorded the audio first and then just lipsynced myself speaking. Oh well, at least I learned to speak better and more natural English in front of a camera. I also learned how to look more sensible and not boring on video. I also filmed my instruments and tried making the video as entertaining as possible.
